How to Install Kali Linux

Step-by-Step Guide

For Dual Boot Installation

Requirements

  • 64-bit Computer
  • Minimum 4 GB RAM (8 GB Recommended)
  • 40 GB Free Disk Space
  • 8 GB USB Drive
  • Stable Internet Connection
  • Windows 10 or Windows 11 (for Dual Boot)

⚠️ Warning

Always back up your important files before installing Kali Linux. Incorrect partition selection may result in data loss.

  1. Check your device compatibility first. Right-click on This PC, select Properties, and check your RAM and whether your system is 32-bit or 64-bit.
  2. Create at least 40 GB of free (unallocated) disk space for Kali Linux. For detailed instructions.. Click here to learn how to create a partition.
  3. A USB flash drive with a minimum capacity of 8 GB is required.
  4. Download the Kali Linux Installer by clicking here: kali.org
  5. Download Rufus by clicking here: rufus installer
  6. After downloading, open Rufus, allow administrator permission, select the Kali Linux ISO file, click Start, and accept all prompts.
  7. Once the process is complete, close all windows, restart your computer, and press F12 (or your computer's boot key) to boot from the USB drive.
  8. Select English as the language and India as the region.
  9. Very Important: Choose the Manual partition option, select the 40 GB free space, verify it carefully, and continue with the installation.
